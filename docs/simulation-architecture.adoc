= Simulation Architecture: Components & Scheduling
:toc: left

Rough design sketch. Not a formal component spec. Covers the engine's general scheduling architecture — discrete-event heap, cell-sets, self-slotting, message bus, fast-forward — and how both physical and anthropogenic (civilisation) components fit it. There is no separate generation/spin-up/runtime split. Every component is self-slotted in the scheduler from epoch (clock = 0); some have a one-shot cadence (effectively "compute once at epoch, then dormant until forced"), others tick continuously at their natural cadence.

NOTE: Urd simulates an arbitrary space body, not Earth. No component assumes Earth-specific conditions. Parameters drive all physical outcomes; Earth is one possible configuration, not the reference.

---

== Engine architecture

The mechanisms below apply uniformly to every component, physical or anthropogenic.

=== Scheduler: discrete-event heap

Each scheduled job declares a cadence in simulated-time units and a next-due time. The scheduler is a min-heap keyed by next-due time:

- Pop the job(s) due soonest.
- Advance simulated time to that point.
- Run the job's DynaCore tick.
- If cadenced, reschedule at `now + cadence`. One-shot jobs do not reschedule.

This naturally handles wildly different cadences (e.g. Tectonics at geological cadence vs Atmosphere at sub-rotational cadence) without wasted iterations — slow components simply sit at the bottom of the heap.

=== Cell-sets: a DynaCore's input is a set of cells

A DynaCore's contract is "given this cell-set (plus boundary/neighbour state for cells just outside it), advance it." Global, regional, and single-cell ticks are all the same operation at different cell-set sizes — no special-casing in the component.

This reframes "is River network global or local": when ticking over a region, the cell-set passed to River is that region's drainage basin(s) (derived from existing basin-membership state), not the whole planet. Basins outside scope are simply not included for that tick — not specially excluded, just not part of the job.

At epoch, all components' cell-sets are global (full body) — there is no focus yet.

=== Self-slotting components

Each component owns its own scheduling:

- At init, registers its epoch job(s) with the scheduler per its cadence, cell-set = global. Continuous jobs reschedule themselves at `now + cadence` on completion.
- Subscribes to `FocusPointChanged` on the message bus. On receipt, derives a cell-set from the new focus (its own domain-specific relevance logic — halo region for field-based components, basin membership for River network, etc.) and registers a **one-shot, higher-priority job** for that cell-set.

No central actor decides cell-sets or cadences on a component's behalf — each component knows its own domain well enough to answer "what's relevant given this focus" and "what's my natural pace."

The cadenced job keeps running on its own schedule regardless of focus, so global/slow state (Tectonics, accumulated erosion, drainage history) is always advancing at its natural pace independent of where the player is looking. The focus-triggered job is additive — it gives the player fresher data for their region sooner, not the only source of truth for that region.

=== Message bus

Pub/sub transport for two kinds of events:

- **`FocusPointChanged(resolution, cell_id, ring_size)`** _(arguments TBD)_ — emitted by the camera/viewport or player agent on movement. Components subscribe and self-slot a one-shot job in response. Absent at epoch (no focus until Civilisation activates or an observer viewport exists).
- **Forcings** — event-driven (player action, civilisation emissions, volcanic event, etc.), not focus-driven. Components subscribe to the forcing types relevant to them. For sustained forcings, the DynaCore keeps a list of active forcings as part of its own state, rather than the bus tracking duration.

NOTE: Profiling is essentially free via this bus — each job emits a start/end event around its tick (component, cell-set size, timestamps). A profiling listener subscribes to these without any component being aware of it. No dedicated instrumentation needed.

=== Deferred: scheduling optimization

Self-slotting can produce redundant or near-redundant jobs — e.g. a focus-triggered job for a region that the cadenced job is about to cover anyway, or two focus changes in quick succession each scheduling overlapping work. Deduplication, merging, or cancellation of redundant jobs is an **optimization problem, not a structural one**, and is explicitly deferred:

- Build the scheduler without any dedup/merge logic first. Redundant ticks are correctness-preserving (worst case: wasted work, not wrong results).
- Only design dedup/merge mechanisms once profiling shows redundancy is a measurable cost.

=== Fast-forward: folding N ticks into fewer

When the clock advances by a large Δ, a cadenced job may be due many times over (N pops) before `now`. Whether those N ticks can fold into fewer (Y < N) is a per-component decision:

- **Re-derivable components** (state is a pure function of slow inputs + current time, no memory): on being popped, compute state directly for the current clock time rather than for the stale `next-due`, then reschedule from there. N pops collapse to 1.
- **History-dependent components** (state depends on the path taken, not just current inputs): cannot fold: must step through intermediate ticks at their own cadence regardless of how large Δ is.

The fold decision and mechanism live in the component/DynaCore, not the scheduler — only the component knows which category it falls into. The scheduler simply advances the clock and hands the current time to whichever job is due; folding (if any) happens inside that job's tick.

== Components

Each entry: cadence (one-shot or continuous), cell-set scope at epoch, and forcing behaviour. "One-shot" components self-slot a single job at epoch and do not reschedule unless a forcing makes them dormant-reactive.

=== Stellar

* **Cadence:** one-shot, at epoch.
* **Produces:** star properties — luminosity, type. Drives all thermal baselines downstream.

=== Planetary

* **Cadence:** continuous, per tick (fastest cadence — primary clock for all irradiance-dependent components).
* **Produces:** celestial position and rotation state, consumed by all irradiance-dependent components. Rotation direction (prograde/retrograde) is an explicit parameter; affects Coriolis direction and ocean gyre orientation downstream.

=== Tectonics

* **Cadence:** continuous, geological cadence (baseline seed at epoch: plate seeds, initial crust type, fault map, volcanism map; subsequent ticks: stress accumulation, orogeny, elevation refinement).
* **Forcing-reactive:** elevation adjustments, volcanic events, can be triggered out of cadence by forcings.

=== Elevation

* **Cadence:** one-shot at epoch (crustal thickness + noise → absolute elevation field), refined by Tectonics and Erosion ticks thereafter.

=== Thermal baseline

* **Cadence:** one-shot at epoch. Per-cell irradiance from stellar output × planetary position × latitude × axial tilt. Static map; atmospheric redistribution handled by Atmosphere.

=== Hydrology

* **Cadence:** continuous, per tick. Epoch tick distributes total water volume (parameter) across the elevation field → ocean basin assignment, land/ocean mask. Subsequent ticks propagate precipitation through the river DAG → river flux, lake levels.

=== Glaciation _(tentative)_

* **Cadence:** continuous, orbital/axial cadence. Ice sheet advance and retreat; major driver of erosion and sea level change. Relationship to Atmosphere not yet resolved.

=== Erosion

* **Cadence:** continuous, century cadence. Water and ice reshape elevation; feeds Elevation and River network on next tick.

=== Biome

* **Cadence:** continuous, decade cadence. Temperature + moisture + soil → vegetation and soil quality map.
* **Forcing-reactive:** rebalances on sustained atmospheric or hydrology shift, or land-use/anthropogenic forcing.

=== Ocean circulation

* **Cadence:** continuous, multi-rotational-period cadence. Thermal gradient → surface current equilibrium. Produces warm/cold coastal masks.

=== River network

* **Cadence:** continuous, rotational-period cadence for flux propagation.
* **Forcing-reactive:** DAG recomputation triggered by elevation change or drainage threshold crossing.

=== Atmospheric circulation

* **Cadence:** continuous, sub-rotational-period cadence. Coriolis + orography → wind field, rain shadow map, moisture distribution, precipitation field. Owns global atmospheric gas composition (CO2, sulfur particulates).
* **Forcing-reactive:** gas composition responds to anthropogenic emission forcings.

=== Isostatic rebound

* **Cadence:** forcing-triggered only. Elevation adjustment triggered by glacial load change.

=== Civilisation

* **Cadence:** not present at epoch; comes online at player-chosen start time. Once active, ticks at its own cadence and is the source of `FocusPointChanged` events (or focus is driven by camera/observer if the player does not materialise as an agent).
* **Consumes at activation:** elevation, river flux, soil quality, temperature, precipitation, biome class (per-cell).
* **Forcings received:** volcanic, flood, drought, glacial.
* **Forcings produced:** land-use change, anthropogenic CO2/pollutant emissions — applied to Atmosphere (gas composition) and Biome (soil quality, vegetation).

== Sequencing

1. Implement scheduler as a discrete-event heap with cadenced + one-shot jobs, no dedup/merge.
2. Implement components above on real backends, each self-slotting per the model.
3. Profile at a realistic resolution/cadence combination.
4. Address scheduling redundancy only where profiling shows it's needed, informed by measured costs rather than speculation.
