# Norse Naming Map

| Name             | Role                       | Mythological grounding                                                                    |
|------------------|----------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Yggdrasil**    | Umbrella project           | The world-tree that survives Ragnarök; the structure that persists across universe deaths |
| **Urtharbrunnr** | Engine / framework         | The Well of Urð at Yggdrasil's roots; the source that feeds everything above it           |
| **Norns**        | Domain models / components | The three weavers of fate who draw from the well; active shapers of simulation state      |
| **Skald**        | Front-end                  | The storyteller; the visible, narrative-facing layer                                      |
| **Edda**         | Savegame                   | The written record of one world's story; one universe iteration                           |

The mythology of the Norns as the conceptual foundation of **Edda**.

Romanisation convention: ð → th (e.g. *Urðr* → Urth, *Verðandi* → Verthandi).

---

## Naming

- **Edda** — the project. The collection of tales, the whole myth-making engine. Each simulation run is a new tale added to the collection.
- **Skald** — the Godot application. The Norse poet-storyteller who narrates the wyrd for the audience. The Skald looks into the Edda and tells what it finds.
- **Urtharbrunnr** (*Urðarbrunnr*) — the Well of Urðr, beneath Yggdrasil, where the three Norns assemble. The former project name; retained as the name of the well in the mythology.

The mise en abyme: the player acts, Odin lives it, the Skald tells it.

---

## The Loom

The Norns weave the web of wyrd (*vefr*) at the loom. The threads are the fates of agents. The web is the product — the emergent tapestry of a civilization over time.

---

## The Three Norns

### Urth — *Urðr* — what happened

The well. The source and the record. What is fixed, what cannot be undone.

### Verthandi — *Verðandi* — what is happening

The active weaving. The present moment in motion.

### Skuld — *Skuld* — what ought to happen

Not the neutral future — the obligated future. What is owed, what must come to pass. *Skuld* shares its root with the English *should*.

---

## The Player

The player is **Odin** — not bound to a single cycle. Odin dies at Ragnarök (killed by Fenrir), the world is reborn, and the player iterates. The avatar wears a different face each cycle; the player is the constant above the myth.

Ragnarök is not a game over screen. It is a transition — the world collapses, is reborn, and the simulation continues with the memory of what came before. Each run is not a reset but an iteration: a spiral, not a circle. The new world is shaped by the wyrd of the old.

Time goes forward only. Odin does not rewind — he endures and adapts.

---

## The Sun and Moon

The Sun and Moon are not realms — they are named mythological entities.

**Sól** (*Sōl*, pronounced like English "sole") rides her chariot across the sky, pursued by the wolf **Sköll** who devours her at Ragnarök. That *Sól* is also the Latin word for the Sun is either coincidence or shared Proto-Indo-European origin — either way, too apt to ignore.

**Máni** is her brother, the Moon, similarly chased by the wolf **Hati**.

## The Nine Realms

The simulation models arbitrary space bodies, not Earth specifically. The nine realms map loosely to bodies in the solar system — each a world with its own characteristics, its own parameter set, its own story.

| Realm            | Space body                 | Character                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
|------------------|----------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Alfheim**      | TBD                        | Realm of the light elves, more beautiful than the sun. Located above Midgard, between Midgard and Asgard. Ruled by Freyr, given as a tooth-gift. Almost absent from the actual myths — a taxonomic entry more than a living realm. Contrasted with Svartalfheim below.                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| **Asgard**       | TBD                        | Realm of the Æsir, located above Midgard. Connected to Midgard by Bifröst — the rainbow bridge, guarded by Heimdall. Contains Valhalla (Odin's hall of the battle-slain), Fólkvángr (Freyja's field), Glaðsheimr (the council hall), and others. The Æsir ride over Bifröst daily to hold council at Urtharbrunnr. Surrounded by a great wall. Bifröst shatters at Ragnarök under the sons of Muspel.                                                                                            |
| **Hel**          | TBD                        | Realm of the inglorious dead — those who die of age, illness, or any death not in battle. Located beneath Yggdrasil, downward and northward. Ruled by Hel, daughter of Loki and Angrboða — half living flesh, half corpse. Not a place of punishment; grey, cold, dim — the absence of life rather than active suffering. Exception: Náströnd, a shore for oath-breakers and murderers, where Níðhöggr feeds. Baldur resides here after his death. Guarded by the dog Garmr on the road Helvegr. |
| **Jotunheim**    | Saturn                     | Realm of the Jotnar, located in the east, beyond Midgard's protective wall. Separated from Asgard by the river Ífingr, which never freezes. Cold, wild, mountainous, hostile. The Jotnar are primordial forces older than the Æsir — many gods have Jotun blood. Odin's mother is a Jotun. Thor's mother is a Jotun. Loki is a Jotun. Home of Utgard-Loki and the stronghold Utgard. Contains Niflheim (Enceladus) within its gravity.                                                           |
| **Midgard**      | Earth                      | Centre of the human world. Life, civilisation.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| **Muspelheim**   | Io (moon of Jupiter)       | Primordial fire, south of Ginnungagap. The most volcanically active body in the solar system — hundreds of active volcanoes, sulfur plains, lava lakes, driven by Jupiter's tidal forces. Home of Surtr, whose fire both created the stars and will end the world.                                                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| **Nidavellir**   | Pluto                      | Realm of the dwarves specifically, distinct from Svartalfheim in Snorri's account. "Nida" relates to darkness or the waning moon. Almost no geography or story attached — essentially a name. The MCU separated it from Svartalfheim, which is arguably cleaner than the sources.                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| **Niflheim**     | Enceladus (moon of Saturn) | Primordial ice and mist, north of Ginnungagap. Geysers of water ice erupting from its south pole, feeding Saturn's E-ring. Hvergelmir — the spring from which all rivers flow.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| **Svartalfheim** | TBD                        | Realm of the dark elves (Dökkálfar), located underground beneath the roots of Yggdrasil. Black, subterranean, lightless. Home of the master craftsmen who forged Mjölnir, Gungnir, Draupnir, Gleipnir, and Skidbladnir. Sources conflate dark elves and dwarves inconsistently.                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| **Vanaheim**     | TBD                        | Realm of the Vanir — gods of fertility, nature, wealth, and seiðr. Older than or contemporaneous with the Æsir. Located in the west. The Æsir-Vanir war ended in truce and hostage exchange, not conquest — Njörðr, Freyr, and Freyja went to Asgard; Hœnir and Mímir to Vanaheim. Freyja takes half the battle-slain before Odin gets his pick.                                                                                                                                                 |

_Yes, that is ten entries in the Nine Realms. We know how to count. The sources are vague and inconsistent on this point. To reduce to nine, the options are: merge Svartalfheim and Nidavellir (dwarves and dark elves are conflated in most sources anyway), or merge Niflheim and Hel (both cold, northward, beneath Yggdrasil, and Snorri is not always consistent in keeping them separate)._
