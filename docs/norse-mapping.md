# Norse Mapping

The mythology of the Norns as the conceptual foundation of **Edda**.

Romanisation convention: ð → th (e.g. *Urðr* → Urth, *Verðandi* → Verthandi).

---

## Naming

- **Edda** — the project. The collection of tales, the whole myth-making engine. Each simulation run is a new tale added to the collection.
- **Skald** — the Godot application. The Norse poet-storyteller who narrates the wyrd for the audience. The Skald looks into the Edda and tells what it finds.
- **Urtharbrunnr** (*Urðarbrunnr*) — the Well of Urðr, beneath Yggdrasil, where the three Norns assemble. The former project name; retained as the name of the well in the mythology.

The mise en abyme: the player acts, Odin lives it, the Skald tells it.

---

## The Loom

The Norns weave the web of wyrd (*vefr*) at the loom. The threads are the fates of agents. The web is the product — the emergent tapestry of a civilisation over time.

---

## The Three Norns

### Urth — *Urðr* — what happened

The well. The source and the record. What is fixed, what cannot be undone.

### Verthandi — *Verðandi* — what is happening

The active weaving. The present moment in motion.

### Skuld — *Skuld* — what ought to happen

Not the neutral future — the obligated future. What is owed, what must come to pass. *Skuld* shares its root with the English *should*.

---

## The Player

The player is **Odin** — not bound to a single cycle. Odin dies at Ragnarök (killed by Fenrir), the world is reborn, and the player iterates. The avatar wears a different face each cycle; the player is the constant above the myth.

Ragnarök is not a game over screen. It is a transition — the world collapses, is reborn, and the simulation continues with the memory of what came before. Each run is not a reset but an iteration: a spiral, not a circle. The new world is shaped by the wyrd of the old.

Time goes forward only. Odin does not rewind — he endures and adapts.

---

## The Sun and Moon

The Sun and Moon are not realms — they are named mythological entities.

**Sól** (*Sōl*, pronounced like English "sole") rides her chariot across the sky, pursued by the wolf **Sköll** who devours her at Ragnarök. That *Sól* is also the Latin word for the Sun is either coincidence or shared Proto-Indo-European origin — either way, too apt to ignore.

**Máni** is her brother, the Moon, similarly chased by the wolf **Hati**.

## The Nine Realms

The simulation models arbitrary space bodies, not Earth specifically. The nine realms map loosely to bodies in the solar system — each a world with its own characteristics, its own parameter set, its own story.

| Realm | Space body | Character |
|---|---|---|
| **Midgard** | Earth | Centre of the human world. Life, civilisation. |
| **Muspelheim** | Mercury | Primordial fire. Brutal temperature swings — 430°C day, -180°C night. Not tidally locked; 3:2 spin-orbit resonance. |
| **Niflheim** | Oort cloud | Primordial ice and mist. Not a single body — a vast cold void at the edge of everything. |
| **Asgard** | Jupiter | Above all. King of planets, king of gods. |
| **Jotunheim** | Saturn | Giants, chaos, untameable. Massive, ringed with debris, storms larger than Earth. |
| **Vanaheim** | Venus | Beauty, the sister pantheon. Radiant from afar, hellish up close — crushing pressure, acid clouds, 460°C. |
| **Alfheim** | Uranus | Light elves, radiance, ethereal. Pale blue, serene, featureless. |
| **Svartalfheim** | Neptune | Dark elves, hidden. Dark, deep, nearly invisible from Earth. |
| **Hel** | Mars | Neither living nor dead. The almost-Earth, almost-habitable, suspended between life and death. |
| **Nidavellir** | Pluto | The dwarves. A dwarf planet. Too clean to ignore. |
